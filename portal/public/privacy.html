PILK

Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 22, 2026

PILK is a personal AI operating system run by Skyway Media on behalf of a single operator. It connects to third-party services — Google, Slack, LinkedIn, X (Twitter), Meta (Facebook / Instagram), and similar providers — so the operator can read from and post to those services through a unified dashboard. This policy describes what data PILK collects, how it is stored, and how to request deletion.

1. Who runs PILK

PILK is operated by Aaron Pilk / Skyway Media. Questions about this policy should be sent to aaron@skyway.media.

2. What data we collect

When you sign in to a third-party service through PILK, the provider issues an OAuth token that authorizes PILK to act on your behalf. Specifically we may collect and store:

  • OAuth access and refresh tokens for each connected account, along with their expiry and granted scopes.
  • Basic profile information returned by each provider after sign-in (name, email or user handle, profile ID, profile picture URL).
  • Content you explicitly ask PILK to read, summarize, or post — for example, messages, posts, or conversations that the operator has PILK analyze or send.
  • Operational metadata — timestamps, tool names, costs, and audit logs of which actions PILK performed on your behalf.

PILK does not collect data beyond what you have explicitly authorized through a connected account or a direct instruction.

3. How the data is used

Data collected through connected accounts is used only to:

  • Execute the tasks the operator instructs PILK to perform (drafting, posting, scheduling, retrieving messages, etc.).
  • Provide context to PILK's internal memory so it can pick up threads across sessions.
  • Show the operator an audit log of PILK's activity for cost, approval, and debugging purposes.

PILK does not sell, rent, or trade user data. It does not share collected data with advertisers or data brokers.

4. How the data is stored

OAuth tokens and related metadata are stored in a private database controlled by Skyway Media. Tokens are stored at rest and transmitted only over HTTPS. Only the operator and personnel under direct Skyway Media control can access them.

PILK's AI components (for example, Anthropic's Claude API) may process content you hand to them in order to generate a reply; those providers operate under their own privacy policies and do not retain prompts for training.

5. Data retention and deletion

Connected-account data is retained until you disconnect the account or request deletion. You can:

  • Disconnect any connected account from the PILK dashboard at Settings → Accounts, which immediately revokes PILK's copy of the token.
  • Revoke PILK's access directly from the provider (for example, Facebook's Business Integrations settings for Meta, or Google Account permissions).
  • Request full deletion of any data associated with your account by following the Data Deletion Instructions or by emailing aaron@skyway.media. We respond to every verified request within 30 days.

6. Security

Access to PILK's systems requires authenticated access. All network traffic is over HTTPS. OAuth tokens are stored in a protected database with restricted filesystem access. We follow industry-standard practices, but no system is perfectly secure; in the unlikely event of a breach affecting your data, we will notify you promptly.

7. Children's privacy

PILK is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect data from anyone under 13. If you believe we have collected such data, please contact us and we will delete it.

8. Changes to this policy

If we materially change this policy we will update the "Last updated" date at the top and, where feasible, notify connected operators by email.
